The Architect

From enterprise innovation to offensive security mastery

Origin Story

My career began in the backend infrastructure of one of India's largest banks. Five years managing production environments, leading deployment teams, solving multi-million dollar infrastructure crises.

I was handpicked by the President/CTO from 1,000+ employees to join an elite innovation team. As the sole technology representative, I architected the bank's first mobile application—establishing its Google Play Store presence and presenting directly to the Board of Directors.

A corporate restructuring ended my tenure. This wasn't a setback—it was a catalyst.

The Deep Dive (2011-2024)

For over a decade, I operated as an independent security specialist. Instead of climbing the corporate ladder, I descended into the technical trenches.

300+ HackTheBox machine compromises. Bug bounty programs on HackerOne and BugCrowd. Private red team labs. Continuous certifications. Independent security consulting under NDA.

This wasn't a gap. It was a decade-long deep dive into understanding how systems break—not theoretically, but practically.

The Breakthrough

Over Christmas weekend 2025, I discovered 14 vulnerabilities using AI-augmented methodology. Ten received federal CVE assignments (71% success rate—more than double the industry average).

Published by Packet Storm Security. Staff praised the work as "driving a bus through this."

Why Syntropy Security

The name represents order emerging from chaos. Legacy systems in healthcare and finance are chaotic—built before security frameworks matured, making dangerous assumptions about user behavior.

Syntropy Security exists to find architectural failures before attackers do. We don't run automated scanners and deliver 500-page reports. We analyze business logic, identify unvalidated assumptions, and systematically break systems.

The Mission

Democratizing vulnerability research. The cybersecurity industry has convinced students that career success requires expensive certifications. I'm proving there's an alternative path.

Earning a federal CVE credential costs nothing but methodology and discipline. I teach students how to find their first CVE within 90 days using freely available tools and systematic analysis.

No gatekeeping. No expensive courses. Just methodology shared openly.
